What Amazon Actually Said

Amazon's 3.5% fuel and logistics surcharge went live on April 17, 2026, applying to Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) in the US and Canada, and Remote Fulfillment with FBA into Canada, Mexico, and Brazil. Starting May 2, 2026, the surcharge extends to Buy with Prime and Multi-Channel Fulfillment (MCF).

On average, this equates to $0.17 per unit for US FBA and CAD $0.26 per unit for Canada FBA, varying by product size and dimensions. Amazon has updated their Revenue Calculator, Profit Analytics, and Fee and Economics Preview reports accordingly.

VASO Group Perspective

Amazon explicitly stated this surcharge is meaningfully lower than what other major carriers are implementing. That's worth noting — but it doesn't change the math for your business. The time to plan was before April 17th. If you're reading this now, the time to act is today.

Why This Matters More Than You Think

At first glance, $0.17 per unit sounds manageable. Scale it across thousands of units per month and the picture changes fast.

This is not an attack on sellers — Amazon absorbed elevated logistics costs for some time before implementing this. But as of April 17th, your margin structure changed. Your strategy needs to adapt.

5 Things Brand Manufacturers Should Do Right Now

  • 1
    Run Your Numbers in Amazon's Updated Tools
    Log into Seller Central. Pull updated reports from the Revenue Calculator, Profit Analytics, and Fee and Economics Preview. Know your actual dollar exposure by ASIN before making any pricing decisions.
  • 2
    Reassess Your Pricing Architecture
    The surcharge applies to fulfillment fees — not your sale price — so margin gets compressed unless you adjust. Consider modest price increases on affected SKUs, bundling to improve per-unit economics, or shifting thin-margin items to FBM.
  • 3
    Audit MCF and Buy with Prime Separately
    The May 2 extension hits Multi-Channel Fulfillment and Buy with Prime. If Amazon fulfills your DTC site or other channels, that's a separate cost center that needs its own model before the deadline.
  • 4
    Protect Your Buy Box Position
    Cost pressure triggers reactive pricing that hurts Buy Box win rate. Don't let margin pressure push you into pricing yourself out of the algorithm. Work with your account team to find the balance between margin defense and competitive positioning.
  • 5
    Turn This Into a Competitive Advantage
    Smaller, undercapitalized sellers will overreact — raise prices aggressively, pull inventory, or lose Buy Box entirely. Brands with strong account management and data-driven strategy will hold position and gain market share while competitors scramble.
